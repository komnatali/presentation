What is NLP?

TO BE BRIEF, it is a technology used to aid computers to understand the human’s natural language and process it, to get computers closer to a human-level understanding of language.

History

1950 - Alan Turing test

The history of NLP started in 1950s, when Alan Turing published an article titled "Intelligence" which proposed what is now called the Turing test as a criterion of intelligence.

It is a test of a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behaviour equivalent to, or indistinguishable from, that of a human.

During the 1960-1970 some successful NLP systems were developed. It’s time for random facts about them:

1966 - ELIZA

Its most famous script was DOCTOR, a simulation of a Rogerian psychotherapist. The program had only to reflect back the patient's statements and ask non-directional questions.

1970 - SHRDLU (ETAOIN SHRDLU)

The name of this system consists 6 of 12 most frequently used letters.

Chatterbots

1980s - revolution! Machine learning algorithms!

Programmers began to write programs which structured real-world information into computer-understandable data (many chatterbots were written);

Up to the 1980s, most NLP systems were based on complex sets of hand-written rules. And there was a REVOLUTION in this area when machine learning algorithms for language processing were introduced.

What are the techniques used in NLP?

Two main techniques are used: syntactic analysis and semantic analysis.

The 1st is used for understanding how the natural language aligns with the grammatical rules.

The 2nd is used to understand the meaning and interpretation of words and how sentences are structured.

Syntax techniques

Tokenization

Breaking up a text into smaller pieces

It is the act of breaking up a text into pieces such as phrases, words, symbols and other elements, which are called tokens.

Lemmatization

Reducing inflected forms of a word into a single form (lemma)

Examples:

better → good

go, gone, going → go

It’s a process of reducing the various inflected forms of a word into a single form (which is called lemma). It’s used for easier analysis.

To use this technique it is necessary to have detailed dictionaries which the algorithm can look through to link the form back to its lemma.

Stemming

Reducing the inflected words to their root form

It is the process of reducing the inflected words to their root form. Developing a stemmer is far simpler than building a lemmatizer.

If we compare this technique with the previous one, we understnd that is's much easier to develop a stemmer than build a lemmatizer.

Morphological segmentation

It involves dividing words into individual units called morphemes.

POS-tags

“In our last meeting" - noun

"We are meeting again tomorrow” - verb

POS-tags or Part-of-speech tagging. It’s identifying the part of speech for every word.

Here we can find a problem. The word can have more than one part of speech. It depends on the context. That’s why we should use the semantic techniques too.

semantic techniques

Named entity recognition (NER)

Examples of groups: persons, locations, organizations, medical codes, etc.

It’s the process of determining the parts of a text that can be categorized into certain groups.

Ex of groups are: persons, locations, organizations, medical codes, etc.

As you can see, this technique is related with information extraction application of NLP.

Word sense disambiguation

Is Washington a man or a state?

It involves giving meaning to a word based on the context.

Is Washington a man or a state? Or maybe a city on the other side of the continent?

Chunking

"South Africa" > "South" + "Africa"

There is the same term in cognitive psychology and (surprisingly) it means the same.

It’s a process of taking individual () pieces of information and grouping them into bigger.

It’s better to use the phrase “South Africa” as a single word instead of two separate words - ‘South’ and ‘Africa’.
